5GO1 - 5th Gear Override.
Alphapike 1 - Garpike #1. Currently in the possession of Githon, thanks to Maradine. To be fielded on Ragnarok. Answers to the name Acuminous, like long walks in the rain (of the blood of infidels), romantic movies (about devouring souls), and crocheting (with its garpikes). His friends call him Pikey.
American - Mammal, native to the western hemisphere. Scientific name Capcomius Screwedovericus Consumerous.
Beta-Tester - Lucky sonofabitch who deserves to die. Also see Parias, Martog, TeTBCN.
Bitch Tank1 - Fuel tank mounted that puts you over standard weight, with the intention of only using it for travel, jettisoning it upon entry to combat. Common Japanese technique.
Blakkjezus1 - Player's name. Also uses as substitute for "Bejesus", as in "You scared the Blakkjezus out of me!"
Bleeder6 - The name for someone in an LoC Lobby who's name is flashing red, indicating a wavering connection.
Body Checking - Slidestepping into an opponent that is in 5th gear in order to cause them to fall.
Boombox2 - Reference to the Earthshaker VT's EMP weapon, the Gauss. May also reference the VT itself.
Burning the Bitch Tank1 - Sprinting around in 5GO, rapidly wasting the fuel in your Bitch Tank.
Cattle Prod2 - Nickname for the PT.
CB - Cutter Boom.
CBoom (Noun)4 - Close range drill weapon employed by some Jarc VTs.
CBoom/CBoomed (Verb)1 - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a CBoom. See also Drill.
CD'd1 - Clip-Dumped.
CE - Short for Connection Error.
Cheddar/Bringing the Cheddar4 - Artillery fire. A reference to the original LoC trailer, where Fox 4 says "Shall I give them a big hello sir?" "Cheddar coming boys!". Or rather, the subtitles make the HORRIFICALLY inaccurate claim that he says it.
Circle of Doom - A battle consisting of two VTs walking in a circle firing at each other. Frequently begun by Jousting, but less-so as time has gone by.
Cliff Diving2 - Flying headlong off of a cliff for distance, most likely landing on your face. Frequently painful.
CtC - Capture the Container. Identical to Capture the Flag in most respects.
Containerboy1 - Person stuck with the duty of carrying the container in CtC.
Clip-Dumping3 - Hitting the Magazine Change button to replace the current clip with a new one, even if it's not empty, for the purpose of negating the burst reload time and allowing you to shoot multiple times in a row. Particularly devastating when used with a TR, as it allows for almost 6 shots at once, causing 3200 damage.
CoD - Circle of Doom.
Connection Error - The usual.
CrazyWalk1 - The erratic jerky walking movement some VTs exhibit that causes FSS to whip back and forth.
Dance Time -
'Dao1- Short for Hai Shi Dao, the antagonists in the original SB.
Death Blossom6 - Nickname for the Prom Series' MM attack.
Decipher - Clueless newbie name for the Decider.
Doze/Dozed - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a Gardozer shield. Less used than Plow.
Dress - What Bladewing wears. Er, I mean, another nickname for Regal Dress. Rarely used, see Regal.
Drill/Drilled - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a CBoom. See also CBoom.
D-soda pop - Babelfish's translation of Decider. Get it? De-"cider"? HAHAHAHAHA... yeaaah.
Dumper - Someone who Clip-Dumps. You dirty whore.
Earth - Mostly harmless.
ES - Earthshaker, a 3rd Gen VT. Yes, the one with the Boombox.
Face - Nickname for the Scare Face VTs.
Faced9 - Just like Vitzhed, but with a Scare Face.
FLHW3 - Forward Launch Howitzer. An exploit in SB that allows you to fire Howitzer artillery weapons forward. Works similarly to the MFLR. Removed from LoC.
Gangbang1 - All-Garpike garpike-only battle.
Gardozer1 - Nickname for the Siegzurg. Reference to it's large bulldozer-like shield, and it's Garpike-like behavior.
Garpike (Proper Noun) - Huge spiky robot of screaming metal death sustained by the souls of newbies. Frequently seen in MotoGP going backwards.
Garpike/Garpikes (Noun)1 - Bang Needles. Not to be confused with Garpikes, the plural of the VT.
Garpike/Garpiked (Verb)1 - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with garpikes. See also Pike.
Hell Week1 - R1T1, where the HSD Vitzh and Vortex were faced with constant assault from the Decider Bitch Rush. This is where true Vitzh Ninja are born .
HSD4 - Hai Shi Dao. Antagonists from the original SB. They have ninjas. The kind that flip-out and kill people.
Inaba - Atsushi Inaba, producer of SB and LoC, among other things. Frequently seen sporting a leather jacket. Also Githon's mortal enemy. INABAAAAA!!!
Japanese - Crazy-ass cybernetic robots connected to the controller who laugh at the stupid round-eyes at every opportunity, who's entire lives are dedicated entirely to embarrassing the American player-base with their superior skills. Or just people with fewer CEs.
Jarc5 - Short for "Jaralaccs", the mercenary faction from SB and LoC.
Jarhead2 - Yet another nickname for the Jaralaccs. Less common than Jarc. Mainly used by Parias, really.
Jousting - When two VTs run almost dead-on at each other. Decider Bitches occasionally get cute and try to PT you. Almost always breaks off into a Circle of Doom.
Leg/Legged/Legging - Refers to blowing the legs off an enemy VT in order to capture it for yourself.
LoC - Line of Contact.
Love Wagon8 - Sin against nature and bad taste.
Mech Assault - The Antichrist.
MFLR3 - Multi Forward Launch Rockets. An exploit in SB that allows you to fire the MLR artillery weapon forward. Works similarly to the FLHW. Removed from LoC.
MM - Multi-Missile, as equipped by the Prom Series' External Armor.
Mock VT - A VT equipped with the Goddess of War Optional Part. If destroyed, the VTs lost/destroyed count will not change. The VT will not be lost, nor will it be captured if legged. The only change is the Goddess of War equipped will be lost. The VT is essentially a fake.
Money Matching - A match where all the participants deliberately allow expensive VTs to be legged by cheaper VTs in order to make tons of cash with nothing lost to anyone. Cheating.
MotoGP - Garpwnd. Hard.
Newbie - "Garpike Food"
nnyoahfied1 - Excessive useless posting has resulted in a negative sign 'mysteriously' appearing in front of your post count.
Peeping Sphere4 - Brilliant translation work. What the LoC manual calls the Peeping Spear. Yes, I've heard people call it this. It was sad.
Pike/Piked1 - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with garpikes. Less used than Garpike.
Plow/Plowed - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a Gardozer shield. See also Doze.
PT - Plasma Torch.
Premature Ejection - Ejecting immediately before being destroyed in order to steal the kill from your opponent. Annoying. Also, sounds dirty, so it's really funny. "No Premature Ejection" "Yeah, that's what she said!".
PRF4 - Pacific Rim Forces. Protagonists from the original SB.
Prod/Prodded - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a PT. Less used than Torch.
Prom - Prominence. As in the PRF 2nd Gen VTs.
Promdress - Either a Prom or a Regal. Who the hell knows. Rarely used.
Pen Pusher - Pilot relegated due to lack of supply points. A failure.
Quick Brake - The act of shifting into Neutral or Reverse gear and hitting the brake pedal in order to stop very quickly.
R1T1 - Round 1, Turn 1. The very start of the game. Never to return, unless Ragnarok should occur.
Ragnarok1 - Theoretical last day of LoC campaign before everyone's profiles are erased or the servers go down. And lo, the mighty Alphapike doth ride across the battlefield, his mighty pikes gleaming with a red tint from the blood of slain newbie and vet alike, his lips moist from the fresh souls of many a would-be Ninja. The mighty leather jacket-clad Inaba will rise from Valhalla and be struck down by the wrath of Acuminous for his flakey attitude towards his projects, the people rejoicing his fall as the very sky crumbles and the isle of Hai Shi Dao descends into the mighty ocean, swallowed up forever until someone discovers a good way to spoof the game. And the mighty Alphapike shall rest, waiting until once more, he shall rise up for the final battle....
RB - Right Brothers. A guerrilla faction that rudely shows up and takes a fourth of the campaign map. Only appears in LoC.
Regal - Regal Dress.
rhindlism - When you make a long, hard attempt at a double entendre.
Sarge5 - Made of Clay.
SB - Steel Battalion.
SBC - Steel Battalion Central.
Scout4 - Early name for the Sheepdog.
Seaslug - Nickname for the 2nd gen VT, the Siegzug.
Shaker - Short for Earthshaker. What, was that too hard for you?
Slidestep4 - Fancy word for "dodge".
Spy Account - Secondary (and often free) XBL account used soley for the purpose of buying VTs from other factions.
Talk-to-the-hand3 - Exploit in SB where the invincible manipulator arm is used to block enemy projectiles. Removed from LoC.
Tazer - Nickname for the Stun Rod PT.
Teki - Japanese word for "enemy". Frequently confused with "Tekki".
Tekki - Japanese name for Steel Battalion. Usually translated as "Iron Horseman".
TeTism - Named in the honor of TeTBCN, this refers the laughing commentaries, often yet deep that are from their Spanish who translated the English, replaced by their use of Babelfish.
Torch/Torched - Slang for attacking/having attacked someone with a PT. See also Prod. Torch is also the generic term for a melee weapon/attack.
Touch - The acting of someone trading you a VT, then you trading it right back, so you can use it in Free Mode.
Vitzh - See Earth.
Vitzhed9 - The phrase for when a Vitzh has dished out a severe beating. "You've been Vitzhed".
Vitzhikaze1 - When a Vitzh desperately rushes you, sacrificing itself in hope that the fire caused by it's death will do at least 3% armor damage. See also Githon's Technique.
Vitzh Tipper - Newbie. Can't even stay standing with a Vitzh.
VT - Vertical Tank. Those'd be the bigass robots you see running around, son.
Wave/Waving2 - The act of activating the manipulator arm and "firing" it at an opponent.
Whiskey Tango Foxtrot - NATO Phonetic Alphabet Code. Figure it out, slacker.
XRay 24 - Useless teammate who can't do anything. See XRay 3.
XRay 34 - Useless teammate who can't do anything. See XRay 2. Except whinier.
YJ - Yellow Jacket, an RB 2nd Gen VT.
Zenith Black and White TV circa 19586 - Main monitor installed in the Vitzh.
